Sphere

designing mobile interface for online corporate education platform

Sphere is an online corporate education startup that aims to improve employees' functional skills by providing them with livestream courses instructed by field experts. In my UI/UX course, each team is tasked to iteratively design a piece of user experience for a startup's product, without seeing the design of their actual product. With a passion for education technology, my team chose Sphere.

  • Project Type: course team project
  • Time: 3 weeks
  • Role: UX designer
  • Constraint: the design process was mostly dictated by the course assignment, but we added the research section and iterated the prototype more than required

Research - Why Livestream?

We looked for literature that discusses the pros and cons of synchronous and asynchronous online teaching, and we found...

Livestream lessons facilitate learning by fostering an interactive learning enviornment.

Boulos, Maged N Kamel et al. “A synchronous communication experiment within an online distance learning program: a case study.”

However, we had some reservations about live streaming, since our research also shows that students in similar professional education programs prefer recorded lessons for time flexibility.

Smith, Laura J et al. “Large-Scale Asynchronous Online Interprofessional Learning Experience.”

But, due to the constraint of this project, we cannot discuss this product choice with Sphere. Although unsatisfying, we decided to compromise by incorporating livestreams as well as a library of past recorded lessons.

brainstorming & sketching

During our brainstorming session, we came up with various possibilities for the three main pages of the mobile interface: home page, course overview page, and chat page.

Through discussion, we decided to keep these design choices to target Sphere's mission statement:

Livestream Courses

    Notify users on the homepage when courses are livestreaming Provide users with live transcript and chat during livestream

Organization of Material

    Organize lesson recordings by last viewed and date published Provide users with a clear list of assignments in progress both on the homepage and course pages Use progress bars to quantify progress when possible

Networking

    Provide information about contact's occupation, employer, and common courses (if applicable)
